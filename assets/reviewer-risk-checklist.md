# Reviewer Risk Checklist

- What is the most likely overclaim in the title or abstract?
- Is the sampling design strong enough for the stated question?
- Are non-detections treated carefully?
- Are survey effort and detectability handled transparently?
- Are spatial and temporal scales matched to the inference?
- Are protected species locations masked?
- Are references real and verified?
- Would a reviewer ask for a simpler or more conservative analysis?
- What result would change the main conclusion if removed?
- Which statement should be downgraded before submission?
