# Manuscript Potential Rubric

Use this rubric after data readiness and before selecting the writing direction. The goal is to decide what the data can honestly support: low, medium, or high manuscript potential.

Do not treat the grade as journal acceptance prediction. Treat it as a carrying-capacity assessment for a deliverable manuscript.

## Dimensions

Score each dimension from 0 to 2.

| Dimension | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sampling design | unclear, opportunistic, or undocumented | documented but limited or uneven | planned, defensible, and matched to the question |
| Sample size / effort | too small for the intended inference | modest but usable for limited claims | sufficient for the main analysis with uncertainty |
| Data completeness | missing key fields or metadata | usable with gaps/caveats | clean, documented, and reproducible |
| Observation process | detectability/effort ignored where important | partly handled or clearly caveated | explicitly modeled or controlled |
| Method-question fit | method cannot answer the stated question | method supports a narrower question | method fits the intended inference |
| Novelty / literature gap | mostly repeats known local descriptive work | incremental but positionable | clear gap or strong system/method contribution |
| Conservation relevance | weak management meaning | locally useful | clearly linked to decisions, threats, or priorities |
| Reproducibility | no code/workflow or unclear processing | partial code or processing notes | code, data processing, and outputs are traceable |
| Ethics / permits / sensitivity | unknown or problematic | mostly available but needs confirmation | documented and publication-safe |

## Grade Bands

Low potential: 0-7. Do not aim for a high-claim research article. Consider a monitoring note, local conservation report, data descriptor, baseline inventory, methods appendix, or modest short communication.

Medium potential: 8-13. A standard empirical manuscript may be deliverable if the question is narrowed and limitations are explicit. Prioritize careful methods, result cards, and a conservative discussion.

High potential: 14-18. A stronger manuscript may be possible if deep research confirms novelty and the analysis holds. Still avoid overclaiming and verify all citations, assumptions, and sensitivity constraints.

## Required Output

Always output:

- Overall grade and score.
- Strongest supporting evidence.
- Weakest limiting factor.
- Best feasible article type.
- Claims allowed.
- Claims not allowed.
- Missing work that would move the project up one grade.

## Downgrade Rules

Downgrade by one level if any of these are true:

- Sensitive location or permit issues are unresolved.
- The proposed claim is causal but the design has no counterfactual or strong temporal logic.
- Sample size or effort is too low for the planned model.
- Key literature shows the proposed contribution is already well established.
- Detection or classification error is central but ignored.

## Upgrade Rules

Upgrade only when new evidence is added, not by rewriting. Examples:

- Add missing metadata and survey effort.
- Re-analyze with a method that matches the observation process.
- Add validation for automated classifications.
- Add literature positioning that reveals a real gap.
- Add supplementary data, code, or spatial covariates.
