# Journal Package Checklist

Use this before finalizing a manuscript draft package.

## Manuscript

- Journal target contract is complete or provisional items are clearly marked.
- Manuscript/article DOCX has no table of contents unless explicitly required by the target journal or user.
- Title matches evidence strength.
- Abstract states design, sample/effort, method, main result, and bounded conclusion.
- One-sentence argument is explicit and the manuscript sections serve that bounded argument.
- Terminology ledger is applied consistently across text, figures, tables, and supplements.
- Introduction follows field context -> method boundary -> regional foundation -> specific gap -> definitions -> objectives.
- Introduction defines core variables and avoids internal process language.
- Introduction length fits the article type or has a functional explanation in the section-length gate.
- Methods include sampling design, data processing, software/packages, assumptions, and ethics/permits where relevant.
- Results report estimates, uncertainty, sample size/effort, and figure/table references.
- Discussion returns to effect direction, effect size/estimate, and uncertainty for central results.
- Discussion language for human RAI, HMSC/occupancy, and centroid/ordination matches the data and model response.
- Discussion does not exceed evidence strength.
- Discussion and Conclusion length fit the article type or have a functional explanation in the section-length gate.
- Conclusion is concise, bounded, and does not introduce new results.
- Limitations include sampling, detectability, uncertainty, classification error, and generalizability.

## Evidence Traceability

- Data contract is complete enough for all key counts, variables, and model outputs.
- Author-knowledge integration has classified existing manuscript/notes/protocols as adopt, verify, narrow, or exclude.
- Count conflicts are reconciled or excluded from manuscript claims.
- Statistical delivery gate classifies every central result as ready or usable with caveat; needs-analysis results are not central claims.
- Every major result has a result-card ID.
- Every title, abstract, result, discussion, and management claim appears in the claim ledger.
- Claim-ledger statuses are keep, narrow, remove, or needs verification; unresolved items are not silently retained.
- Claim-ledger audit has no blocking issues, or issues are listed as author tasks.
- Reviewer-objection simulation has no open high or fatal objections unless the deliverable is downgraded or the objection is disclosed as a limitation.
- Section-length audit has no unexplained underdeveloped or overextended Introduction, Discussion, or Conclusion warnings.
- Manuscript-language audit has no unresolved process-language or overclaim warnings.

## Figures and Tables

- Study area and sampling design are visible or described.
- Main figures and tables are assembled or attached according to the journal target contract.
- Source data or reproducibility route is mapped for every central figure and table.
- Main results have self-contained captions.
- Units, sample sizes, effort, and uncertainty are present.
- Sensitive coordinates are masked or generalized.
- Supplementary tables are prepared for species list, model coefficients, diagnostics, and variable definitions when needed.

## References

- Standard research-article drafts normally include at least 30 verified relevant sources, or state why the deliverable is narrower.
- Every citation is verified from a real source.
- Reference verification checks title, year, source, DOI/URL, status, and official database/report metadata; citation coverage alone is not enough.
- Every in-text citation has a matching reference-list entry.
- Every reference-list entry is cited in the manuscript body or moved to a separate literature matrix.
- Deep-research sources are assigned a manuscript function or removed from the reference list.
- Method citations are present.
- Software/package citations are present where relevant.
- Species taxonomy and threat/protection status sources are cited.
- Novelty claims and discussion comparisons are backed by specific citations.

## Ethics, Permits, and Data

- Submission metadata contract is complete enough for the intended delivery level.
- Survey permits and ethics statements are provided or marked as author-confirmation needed.
- Data availability statement is present.
- Code availability statement is present.
- Data/code availability routes, source-data mapping, FAIR metadata, and sensitive-data restrictions are documented.
- Sensitive species location policy is stated.
- Conflicts, funding, and author contributions are present or marked for completion.

## AI Use

- AI tool use is disclosed when required.
- AI-generated content, citations, or analysis suggestions were verified by human authors.
- No confidential or unauthorized data were uploaded to external services unless allowed.

## Delivery Level

- Delivery readiness score is assigned: internal scientific draft, reviewable manuscript, journal-format manuscript, or submission-ready package.
- Output is not called submission-ready unless all required metadata, references, figures/tables, sensitive-data handling, and statements are confirmed.
