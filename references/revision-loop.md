# Revision Loop

Use this after producing a first deliverable draft.

## Principle

Improve the manuscript within its evidence grade. Do not upgrade the apparent quality by hiding weak data, overstating novelty, or adding unsupported claims.

## Loop

1. Polish and improve the draft.
2. Evaluate the completed content against manuscript potential, claim boundaries, journal fit, and reviewer risk.
3. Recheck the data contract, result cards, claim ledger, citation coverage, and reviewer-objection simulation.
4. For every medium, high, or fatal reviewer objection, carry forward its route-back gate and repair artifact; do not collapse it into a generic rewrite task.
5. Recheck the journal target contract, submission metadata, author-knowledge integration, statistical delivery gate, figure/table assembly, reference verification, and delivery readiness score.
6. Produce a decision list for the author.
7. Wait for author choices or infer only low-risk edits if the user explicitly asks for automatic revision.
8. Revise again within the confirmed scope.

## Evaluation Categories

Assess:

- Is the title too strong?
- Does the abstract match actual evidence?
- Does the manuscript still serve one bounded one-sentence argument?
- Are canonical terms consistent with the terminology ledger?
- Does the Introduction have a specific gap, operational definitions, and objective-to-method alignment?
- Does the Introduction length fit the article type, or is any short/long form functionally justified?
- Are Methods reproducible?
- Have confirmed author-provided local details been integrated without inheriting overclaims?
- Do Results all trace to result cards?
- Are central analyses classified as ready or usable with caveat by the statistical delivery gate?
- Do all title, abstract, result, discussion, and management claims appear in the claim ledger?
- Do key numbers and variables trace back to the data contract?
- Does Discussion return to effect size/uncertainty for central results?
- Does Discussion avoid over-interpreting non-significant human RAI, HMSC, occupancy, centroid, or ordination outputs?
- Does Discussion compare nearest literature before making implications?
- Does Discussion length reflect result-linked interpretation rather than padding or speculation?
- Do in-text citations, deep-research sources, and the reference list pass citation coverage?
- Have central references been verified for title, year, source, DOI/URL, and status?
- Does the manuscript-language audit flag process language or over-strong causal wording?
- Are high or fatal reviewer objections addressed, disclosed, or converted into downgrade decisions?
- Are figures, tables, captions, and supplements assembled for the intended delivery level?
- Are data/code availability routes and source-data mappings complete for the intended delivery level?
- Does the DOCX avoid a table of contents unless explicitly required?
- Is the delivery readiness level stated honestly?
- Are limitations honest and specific?
- Are data/code/ethics/permits/AI-use statements complete?
- Is the selected article type realistic?

## Author Decision List

Group suggestions into:

- Must do: errors, missing methods, unsupported claims, absent permissions, fake/unverified citations, uncited reference-list entries, in-text citations missing from references.
- Evidence traceability: unresolved data-contract facts, missing result-card links, missing claim-ledger entries, unsupported claim strengths.
- Argument/terminology: unstable central argument, inconsistent terms, undefined abbreviations, or paragraph jobs that hide unsupported claims.
- Delivery blockers: missing journal target contract, unresolved submission metadata, unassembled figures/tables, unverified central references, accidental table of contents, or overstated delivery readiness level.
- Data availability blockers: missing data/code route, missing source data for central figures/tables, unclear sensitive-data restrictions, or absent repository/access process.
- Reviewer route-back tasks: medium/high/fatal objections grouped by owning gate and required repair artifact.
- Section balance: unexplained underdeveloped or overextended Introduction, Discussion, or Conclusion.
- Should do: better figures, clearer limitations, stronger nearest literature, sensitivity checks.
- Could do: stylistic polish, alternative title, improved captions, supplemental framing.
- Do not do: changes that would inflate claims beyond evidence.

## Revision Rule

When revising, explicitly state whether the change:

- improves clarity,
- improves evidence traceability,
- narrows claims,
- adds verified support,
- or requires new analysis/data.

Only the first four can be done immediately. New analysis/data requires author action.
