# Citation Coverage Check

Use this after drafting and before finalizing. The purpose is to make sure deep research, in-text citations, and the reference list form a complete evidence loop.

## Principle

Do not use references as decoration. Every citation in the reference list should serve a manuscript function, and every major background, method, comparison, or management claim should be traceable to either data or a cited source.

For a standard empirical research article, the manuscript should normally be supported by at least 30 verified relevant sources before final drafting. A lower count is acceptable only for a deliberately narrower short communication, data note, monitoring report, or local management report.

## Required Checks

### 1. Source-to-Manuscript Coverage

For every deep research source, record whether it is used.

| Source | Function | Used in text? | Location | Keep / remove / cite later |
|---|---|---|---|---|

Remove sources that are not used unless they are intentionally kept in a separate literature matrix.

### 2. In-Text-to-Reference Coverage

For every in-text citation, ensure a matching reference-list entry exists.

Check:

- Author-year spelling.
- Chinese author names and year.
- Software/package citation.
- Online report citation.
- In press / ahead-of-print status.
- DOI or URL when needed.

### 3. Reference-to-In-Text Coverage

For every reference-list entry, ensure it appears in the manuscript body.

If not:

- Add a relevant in-text citation, or
- Move it to a literature matrix, or
- Remove it from the manuscript reference list.

### 4. Claim Support Coverage

Every major claim needs one of:

- A result card.
- A figure/table.
- A verified citation.
- A marked author-confirmation task.

High-risk unsupported claims include:

- Novelty claims: "first", "rarely studied", "few studies".
- Causal claims: "caused", "driven by", "resulted in".
- Mechanism claims: "because", "due to", "indicates habitat quality".
- Management claims: "effective", "should", "priority".
- Method-standard claims: occupancy, density, abundance, detectability, model assumptions.

## Minimum Passing Standard

A manuscript draft passes only if:

- Standard research-article drafts normally have at least 30 verified relevant sources or an explicit downgrade rationale.
- Introduction uses enough sources to support the background, method boundary, regional foundation, and specific gap, normally 10-15 sources for a standard article.
- Discussion compares every major result with nearby literature before drawing broader implications.
- Methods citations are present for major analytical tools.
- Regional/context citations are present for study-system claims.
- Discussion comparison citations are present before broader interpretation.
- No unverified or invented citations are included.
- No reference-list entries are uncited in the main text unless the target journal explicitly allows further reading lists.
- The reference list and in-text citations match in year and author spelling.

## When Using AI

AI-assisted citations must be treated as unverified until checked against original sources, DOI pages, publisher pages, PubMed, Crossref, official reports, or journal websites.
