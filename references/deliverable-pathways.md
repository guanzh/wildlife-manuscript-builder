# Deliverable Pathways

Choose the manuscript path from the potential grade. The target is a deliverable article, not an artificially inflated one.

## Low-Potential Data

Best outputs:

- Monitoring baseline note.
- Species inventory or occurrence update.
- Local conservation report adapted into a short article.
- Data descriptor if metadata can be cleaned.
- Methods or workflow note if the data processing pipeline is useful.
- Negative or limited-evidence note if it answers a clear practical question.

Writing strategy:

- Lead with transparent scope.
- Emphasize study system, monitoring effort, and what the data can document.
- Avoid mechanisms, causal claims, density estimates, and broad generalization.
- Use limitations as part of the contribution: what future monitoring must fix.

Deep research goal:

- Find similar short notes, data papers, baseline reports, and local conservation studies.
- Identify a modest contribution: new locality, time period, species record, monitoring design lesson, or management gap.

## Medium-Potential Data

Best outputs:

- Standard empirical article with narrowed question.
- Short communication with robust core result.
- Habitat association or occupancy paper with careful limitations.
- Activity rhythm or disturbance association paper.
- Management-priority analysis if decision criteria are explicit.

Writing strategy:

- Build around 2-3 result cards.
- Keep the title and abstract aligned with association or evidence language.
- Use discussion to compare nearest studies and explain boundaries.
- Provide clear next-step analyses that could strengthen the paper.

Deep research goal:

- Confirm the nearest literature gap.
- Identify the expected method standard.
- Find target journals or article formats that accept incremental but solid field data.

## High-Potential Data

Best outputs:

- Full empirical article.
- Integrated monitoring or multi-source inference paper.
- Management effectiveness article with defensible design.
- Methodological paper if the analysis pipeline is novel and generalizable.

Writing strategy:

- Let analysis and novelty carry the strength, not adjectives.
- Include robust checks, sensitivity analyses, and transparent uncertainty.
- Use a stronger narrative only after the result cards support it.

Deep research goal:

- Test whether the contribution is genuinely beyond local description.
- Compare with recent high-quality papers.
- Identify likely reviewer objections early.

## Path Selection Prompt

For each possible direction, produce:

- Direction title.
- Best article type.
- Potential grade.
- Required additional work.
- Main risk.
- Why it is or is not deliverable.

Choose the direction with the highest deliverability, not necessarily the highest ambition.
