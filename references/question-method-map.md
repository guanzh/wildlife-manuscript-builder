# Question-Method Map

Use this map to turn a broad wildlife conservation topic into a defensible manuscript route.

| User Question | Scientific Form | Common Methods | Required Evidence | Safer Claim |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Where does the species occur? | Occurrence and occupancy | Occupancy, SDM, spatial GLM/GLMM | Sites, visits/effort, covariates, validation | The species was more likely to be detected/occupy areas with... |
| How many are there? | Abundance or density | SCR, distance sampling, N-mixture, REM/REST, time/space-to-event | Design assumptions, effort, detectability or movement parameters | Estimated density/abundance under stated assumptions... |
| Are populations changing? | Temporal trend | Dynamic occupancy, state-space, GLMM, before-after with effort control | Comparable survey periods and effort | Detection/occupancy/count index changed over... |
| Does disturbance matter? | Disturbance association | Occupancy, GLMM, GAM, multivariate community models | Disturbance metrics, covariates, sampling balance | Occurrence/activity/community metrics were associated with... |
| Are animals avoiding humans in time? | Temporal avoidance | Activity curves, overlap coefficients, circular statistics | Reliable timestamps, human and wildlife events | Activity overlap was lower/higher under... |
| Are species interacting? | Co-occurrence or interaction hypothesis | Two-species occupancy, joint species models, temporal/spatial overlap | Scale-aware co-detection, covariates, alternative explanations | Co-occurrence patterns were consistent with..., not proof of interaction |
| Did a management action work? | Intervention effect | BACI, DID, interrupted time series, matched controls | Treatment/control or defensible counterfactual | Results are consistent with an intervention effect if assumptions hold |
| Where should action be prioritized? | Decision support | Risk mapping, prioritization, cost-effectiveness, threshold models | Risk score, uncertainty, costs, action constraints | Areas ranked higher for priority under explicit criteria |
| Is the habitat intact? | Integrity assessment | Multi-indicator framework, landscape metrics, community indicators | Habitat, species, threats, connectivity, validation | Evidence from monitored indicators suggests... |

## Drafting Order by Evidence Strength

If evidence is descriptive, draft a monitoring baseline, inventory, or short communication.

If evidence controls observation error, draft occupancy, trend, or habitat association.

If evidence supports counterfactual reasoning, draft management effectiveness.

If evidence integrates multiple observation processes, draft a synthesis or integrated model paper.

When the user's preferred question is stronger than the data, lower the question strength rather than inflating the language.
